Barkus Aurelius — Support Dog, Philosopher, and Stoic (Terms and Conditions Apply)

Barkus Aurelius is our wise old soul: a dignified, grey-whiskered hound who approaches life with the calm of a Roman emperor. He meditates. He reflects. He gazes serenely across the garden as though quietly composing a book of gentle wisdom. His composure is legendary — and absolutely, completely void where doorbells apply.

The moment a doorbell rings (or a gate buzzer sounds, or a car door slams in the street), the philosopher vanishes and is replaced, briefly, by a barking whirlwind of pure feeling. A few seconds later order is restored, dignity is recovered, and Barkus returns to his contemplations as though nothing whatsoever has happened.

Between doorbells, Barkus is the most soothing companion imaginable — patient, steady, and endlessly reassuring. He is especially good with clients who need a calm, grounding presence to lean on.

Likes: sunny philosophy on the stoep, long thoughtful walks, and biltong offered with appropriate respect. Dislikes: doorbells, obviously.

Barkus is available to book for sessions. For the smoothest experience, we recommend putting your phone and your gate on silent.
