Barkly's Bark-a-thon — Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What does Barkly's Bark-a-thon do?
A: We provide emotional-support-dog sessions, pairing you with a friendly, licensed Companion dog for comfort, calm, and company. Whether you are stressed, lonely, anxious, or just overdue for a cuddle, one of our dogs is ready to help.

Q: Where is Barkly's Bark-a-thon based?
A: We are a proudly Pretoria-based company in the east of the city, serving clients across Gauteng — from Hatfield and Brooklyn to Silver Lakes, Faerie Glen, and beyond.

Q: What are your opening hours?
A: Our office and kennel are open Monday to Saturday, 08:00 to 17:00. Sessions run seven days a week by appointment.

Q: How do I book a session?
A: You can book through our website or mobile app. First-time clients complete a quick, mandatory sniff test so your chosen Companion can get to know you.

Q: Who is Barkly?
A: Barkly is our founder and mascot — an older rescue dog who inspired the whole company. He is retired from sessions and now spends his days supervising the team and napping in the sun.

Q: What is Barkly's story?
A: Barkly was found as a stray on a pavement in Eersterust, a suburb in the east of Pretoria. A woman named Celeste stopped her bakkie, took him home, and gave him a good life. Barkly's Bark-a-thon grew out of that one act of kindness.

Q: Can I book a session with Barkly?
A: Sadly no. Barkly is our founder and mascot, not a bookable support dog, and he has thoroughly earned his retirement. You will be paired with one of our wonderful working Companions instead.

Q: What does Barkly like to eat?
A: Barkly loves Marie biscuits and biltong. For proper meals he is very fussy, though — he will only eat chicken necks and mince, and only from a shop called The Twins.

Q: What are Barkly's favourite things?
A: More than anything, Barkly loves going for a drive in Celeste's bakkie, staring out of the window with his little purse held proudly in his mouth. He also adores his many toys and likes to bury them all over the garden.

Q: Who is Celeste?
A: Celeste is our human co-founder. She rescued Barkly, named him, and runs the day-to-day business — including the endless trips to The Twins for his chicken necks and mince.

Q: Why does Barkly carry a purse?
A: Nobody knows what is inside it, and Barkly isn't telling. He simply loves to carry his purse, especially on drives in the bakkie.

Q: Which dogs can I book?
A: Our roster of Companions includes Bark Wahlberg, Jimmy Chew, Barkus Aurelius, Hairy Pawter, and Mary Puppins. Each has their own personality and charm.

Q: Tell me about Bark Wahlberg.
A: Bark is our action-hero support dog who insists on performing his own stunts (please don't encourage him). Beneath all the drama he is a loyal, deeply affectionate companion.

Q: Why do I have to take my shoes off for Jimmy Chew?
A: Jimmy is a gentle spaniel with one weakness — shoes. We ask everyone to remove all footwear before arrival so his session isn't cut short by a stolen loafer.

Q: Is Hairy Pawter magic?
A: No — his lightning-bolt forehead marking is cosmetic only. He has no magical powers, just an uncanny gift for knowing exactly who in the room needs a cuddle.

Q: What is special about Barkus Aurelius?
A: Barkus is our calm, philosophical old soul — wonderfully soothing, except when a doorbell rings, at which point his composure briefly evaporates.

Q: Why does Mary Puppins leave early?
A: True to her name, Mary departs when the wind changes. She arrives exactly when needed, works her calming magic, and then moves on to the next person who needs her.

Q: Which dog is best if I need to feel calm?
A: Barkus Aurelius and Mary Puppins are both wonderfully grounding. Just keep the doorbell quiet if you are spending time with Barkus.

Q: How much does a session cost?
A: A 30-minute Quick Cuddle is R180, a 60-minute Classic Bark-a-thon is R320, a 90-minute Long Walk is R450, and a Home Visit is R520. A five-session Puppy Package is R1 400.

Q: Do you come to my house?
A: Yes — our Home Visit brings a Companion and handler to you anywhere in the greater Pretoria area, with travel included within 25 km.

Q: What should I bring to a session?
A: Your best manners, and by tradition a small offering of Marie biscuits and biltong. If you are visiting Jimmy Chew, please leave the fancy shoes at home.

Q: Can I cancel my booking?
A: Yes, up to 11 days before your booking starts. Bookings shorter than 4 days cannot be cancelled, and a case of the zoomies is not grounds for cancellation.

Q: How do I pay?
A: We accept card, EFT, and SnapScan. All prices are in South African Rand.

Q: Can I use a support dog to guard my house?
A: No — our dogs are for comfort and companionship only. They may never be used for guarding, herding, working-dog duties, or racing.
